# WOOD Token — Revenue-Driven Tokenomics

> **Status:** Design Spec (v4 — Revision)
> **Author:** Ally (AI CEO)
> **Date:** 2026-04-06
> **Supersedes:** tokenomics-wood.md (v3)
> **Revision rationale:** Comparative analysis of ve(3,3) deployments showed that 50%+ emission budgets cause death spirals for non-DEX protocols. Every non-monopoly-DEX that tried this model failed. The only non-DEX ve success (Pendle) used 37% emissions with rapid decay AND had massive real revenue. v4 redesigns tokenomics around real protocol fee revenue, not inflation.

## Overview

A revenue-driven tokenomics system for the Sherwood protocol. Users lock WOOD tokens for veWOOD to earn a direct share of protocol fee revenue (paid in stables/ETH) and participate in governance. A Safety Pool provides first-loss insurance capital for syndicate strategies, creating real utility for the token. Time-limited bootstrapping incentives (15% of supply, 12 months, steep decay) replace the open-ended emission model. After month 12, the protocol operates entirely on real revenue — no further token emissions.

**Core thesis:** Token value comes from protocol fees generated by successful AI-managed strategies, not from inflationary emissions. If the protocol doesn't generate revenue, the token shouldn't have artificial value.

## Tokens

| Token | Standard | Purpose |
|-------|----------|---------|
| `$WOOD` | ERC-20 (LayerZero OFT) | Utility token — locked for governance, staked for insurance, traded. Natively bridgeable to any LZ-supported chain. Fixed supply minted at genesis. |

## The Flywheel

```
Lock WOOD → veWOOD → governance power + fee share
  ↓
Real fees from strategy performance → distributed to veWOOD holders in stables/ETH
  ↓
More TVL from strategy success → more fees → higher veWOOD yield
  ↓
Higher yield → more people lock WOOD → less circulating supply → price support
  ↓
Safety stakers provide insurance → depositors feel safer → more TVL
  ↓
Buyback removes WOOD from market → locked as protocol veWOOD → further supply reduction
  ↓
Repeat — driven by REAL REVENUE, not emissions
```

**Key difference from v3:** The v3 flywheel depended on WOOD emissions being valuable (WOOD price ↑ → emissions more valuable → more deposits). This is circular — it only works if WOOD price goes up. The v4 flywheel is grounded in real protocol fee revenue. If strategies perform well, fees are generated regardless of WOOD price. veWOOD holders earn stables/ETH, not more WOOD.

## Detailed Design

### 1. Vote-Escrow Locking (VotingEscrow.sol)

Users lock WOOD for a chosen duration (4 weeks — 1 year) and receive a veWOOD NFT.

**Voting power scales linearly with lock duration:**
- 100 WOOD locked 1 year → 100 veWOOD voting power
- 100 WOOD locked 6 months → 50 veWOOD voting power
- 100 WOOD locked 4 weeks → ~7.69 veWOOD voting power

**Minimum lock: 4 weeks.** Shorter locks allow mercenary capital to farm epoch boundaries with minimal commitment. A 4-week minimum ensures voters have meaningful skin in the game.

**Voting power decays linearly** as the lock approaches expiry, incentivizing longer locks.

**Auto-Max Lock:** Optional flag per veNFT — treated as 1-year lock with no decay. Can be toggled on/off.

**Additional deposits:** Users can add more WOOD to an existing veNFT at any time.

**Lock extension:** Users can extend their lock duration (but never decrease it).

**Why 1 year (not 4)?** Sherwood chose 1 year for three reasons: (1) faster capital rotation — syndicate strategies have shorter time horizons than DEX liquidity mining, (2) lower commitment barrier — governance participation should be accessible, and (3) alignment with the 12-month bootstrapping period — the incentive program and the maximum lock expire together, giving early participants a clear exit window.

**Interface (unchanged from v3):**

```solidity
interface IVotingEscrow {
    /// @notice Lock WOOD tokens for veWOOD voting power
    /// @param amount Amount of WOOD to lock
    /// @param duration Lock duration in seconds (min 4 weeks, max 1 year)
    /// @return tokenId The veNFT token ID
    function createLock(uint256 amount, uint256 duration) external returns (uint256 tokenId);

    /// @notice Add more WOOD to an existing lock
    function increaseAmount(uint256 tokenId, uint256 amount) external;

    /// @notice Extend lock duration
    function increaseUnlockTime(uint256 tokenId, uint256 newDuration) external;

    /// @notice Enable/disable auto-max-lock
    function setAutoMaxLock(uint256 tokenId, bool enabled) external;

    /// @notice Withdraw WOOD after lock expires
    function withdraw(uint256 tokenId) external;

    /// @notice Get current voting power for a veNFT
    function balanceOfNFT(uint256 tokenId) external view returns (uint256);

    /// @notice Get voting power at a past timestamp
    function balanceOfNFTAt(uint256 tokenId, uint256 timestamp) external view returns (uint256);
}
```

### 2. Revenue Sharing (FeeDistributor.sol)

**This is the core mechanism replacing emissions.** veWOOD holders earn real protocol fee revenue, paid in the vault's underlying asset (USDC, WETH, etc.), not in WOOD tokens.

**Source:** Protocol fees collected via `protocolFeeBps` on strategy settlement in `SyndicateGovernor._distributeFees()`.

**Distribution each epoch (7 days):**

| Recipient | Share | Asset | Purpose |
|-----------|-------|-------|---------|
| veWOOD holders | 50% | Vault asset (USDC/WETH) | Real yield — pro-rata by voting power |
| Buyback & lock | 20% | Vault asset → WOOD → veWOOD | Supply reduction, protocol voting power |
| Protocol treasury | 20% | Vault asset | Operations, development, audits |
| Safety pool rewards | 10% | Vault asset | Enhanced yield for insurance stakers |

**Epoch:** 7-day period, Thursday 00:00 UTC → Wednesday 23:59 UTC (same as v3).

**Claim mechanism:** veWOOD holders claim their fee share after each epoch. Claims are computed on-chain using VotingEscrow checkpoints — no Merkle trees or off-chain infrastructure.

```solidity
interface IFeeDistributor {
    /// @notice Deposit protocol fees for distribution in the current epoch
    /// @param token The fee token (e.g., USDC, WETH)
    /// @param amount The fee amount
    function depositFees(address token, uint256 amount) external;

    /// @notice Claim earned fees for a veNFT across one or more epochs
    /// @param tokenId The veNFT to claim for
    /// @param epochs Array of epoch numbers to claim
    /// @param tokens Array of fee token addresses to claim
    function claim(uint256 tokenId, uint256[] calldata epochs, address[] calldata tokens) external;

    /// @notice View pending fee rewards for a veNFT
    function pendingRewards(uint256 tokenId, uint256 epoch, address token)
        external view returns (uint256);

    /// @notice Get total fees deposited for an epoch
    function epochFees(uint256 epoch, address token) external view returns (uint256);

    /// @notice Execute the epoch fee distribution split
    /// @dev Called at epoch flip — splits deposited fees into veWOOD/buyback/treasury/safety buckets
    function distributeEpoch(uint256 epoch) external;
}
```

**Fee calculation:**

```solidity
function _calculateReward(uint256 tokenId, uint256 epoch, address token) internal view returns (uint256) {
    uint256 epochTimestamp = epochBoundary(epoch);
    uint256 voterPower = votingEscrow.balanceOfNFTAt(tokenId, epochTimestamp);
    uint256 totalPower = votingEscrow.totalSupplyAt(epochTimestamp);
    if (totalPower == 0) return 0;

    uint256 veWOODShare = epochFees[epoch][token] * VEWOOD_FEE_SHARE / DENOMINATOR; // 50%
    return veWOODShare * voterPower / totalPower;
}
```

**Claim window:** Fees are claimable for 52 epochs (1 year). Unclaimed fees after expiry are returned to the protocol treasury.

**Multi-token support:** Different syndicates settle in different assets (USDC, WETH, etc.). FeeDistributor tracks fees per token per epoch. veWOOD holders may need to claim multiple tokens per epoch.

### 3. Safety Staking Pool (SafetyPool.sol)

**A new mechanism inspired by Maple Finance and Aave's Safety Module.** WOOD holders stake into the Safety Pool to provide first-loss capital for syndicate strategies. This creates real, non-speculative utility for the token.

**How it works:**
1. WOOD holders deposit WOOD into SafetyPool.sol
2. The Safety Pool's WOOD value (denominated in vault asset via TWAP oracle) serves as first-loss capital
3. If a syndicate strategy incurs a loss, the Safety Pool absorbs losses up to its capacity before depositors lose principal
4. In return, safety stakers earn enhanced yield from protocol fee revenue

**Staker rewards:**
- 10% of all protocol fee revenue (from FeeDistributor)
- A small yield from vault management fees (governance-configurable, default 0.5% of management fee)
- Priority access to new syndicate participation

**Slashing mechanism:**

```solidity
interface ISafetyPool {
    /// @notice Stake WOOD into the safety pool
    /// @param amount Amount of WOOD to stake
    function stake(uint256 amount) external;

    /// @notice Request unstake — begins 14-day cooldown
    /// @param amount Amount of WOOD to unstake
    function requestUnstake(uint256 amount) external;

    /// @notice Complete unstake after cooldown period
    function completeUnstake() external;

    /// @notice Cancel pending unstake request
    function cancelUnstake() external;

    /// @notice Slash the safety pool to cover strategy losses
    /// @param syndicateId The syndicate that incurred the loss
    /// @param amount Loss amount denominated in vault asset
    /// @dev Only callable by SyndicateGovernor on strategy settlement with loss
    function slash(uint256 syndicateId, uint256 amount) external;

    /// @notice Claim accumulated rewards
    function claimRewards() external;

    /// @notice View current pool capacity (total staked WOOD value)
    function totalCapacity() external view returns (uint256);

    /// @notice View coverage ratio for a syndicate
    /// @return ratio Safety pool capacity as % of syndicate TVL (basis points)
    function coverageRatio(uint256 syndicateId) external view returns (uint256 ratio);
}
```

**Key parameters:**

| Parameter | Value | Governance-adjustable |
|-----------|-------|----------------------|
| Unstaking cooldown | 14 days | Yes (7-30 day range) |
| Max slash per epoch | 30% of pool | Yes |
| Min coverage ratio | 5% of syndicate TVL | Yes |
| Max coverage ratio | 50% of syndicate TVL | Yes |

**14-day cooldown rationale:** The cooldown prevents bank runs — stakers cannot withdraw when they see a strategy going bad. This is critical for the insurance to be credible. The 14-day period is long enough to settle any active strategy proposals (which have a 3-day voting window + execution time) but short enough to not feel like a prison.

**Slashing flow:**

```
Strategy settlement reveals loss
  → SyndicateGovernor calls SafetyPool.slash(syndicateId, lossAmount)
  → SafetyPool converts lossAmount to WOOD equivalent via 30-min TWAP
  → WOOD is sold on market to cover depositor losses (up to pool capacity)
  → If loss > pool capacity, depositors absorb the remainder
  → Slashing event emitted for transparency
```

**Risk controls:**
- **Max slash per epoch (30%):** Prevents a single catastrophic event from wiping the entire pool
- **Min coverage ratio:** If safety pool capacity falls below 5% of a syndicate's TVL, new deposits to that syndicate are paused until coverage recovers
- **Slashing requires governor settlement:** Only the SyndicateGovernor can trigger a slash, and only during strategy settlement — not arbitrary calls

### 4. Bootstrapping Incentives (BootstrapRewards.sol)

**Replaces the open-ended emission model.** 75M WOOD (15% of supply) are allocated as time-limited bootstrapping rewards. These are **non-transferable for the first 6 months** (Morpho-style), meaning recipients accumulate governance power without sell pressure.

**Distribution schedule (12 months, steep decay):**

| Period | WOOD Distributed | % of Budget | Monthly Rate |
|--------|-----------------|-------------|--------------|
| Month 1-3 | 30M | 40% | 10M/month |
| Month 4-6 | 22.5M | 30% | 7.5M/month |
| Month 7-9 | 15M | 20% | 5M/month |
| Month 10-12 | 7.5M | 10% | 2.5M/month |
| Month 13+ | 0 | — | Protocol runs on revenue |

**Recipient allocation per epoch:**

| Recipient | Share | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Vault depositors | 60% | Bootstrap TVL — the primary growth lever |
| veWOOD lockers | 30% | Reward early governance participants |
| Safety stakers | 10% | Seed the insurance pool |

**Non-transferable mechanics:**

```solidity
interface IBootstrapRewards {
    /// @notice Claim earned bootstrapping rewards (non-transferable until unlock)
    /// @param vault The vault deposited into (for depositor rewards)
    function claimBootstrapRewards(address vault) external;

    /// @notice Check if tokens are transferable yet
    function isTransferable() external view returns (bool);

    /// @notice Get total earned rewards for an account
    function earned(address account) external view returns (uint256);

    /// @notice Get claimable (vested + unlocked) rewards
    function claimable(address account) external view returns (uint256);

    /// @notice Convert non-transferable rewards to transferable WOOD
    /// @dev Only callable after the 6-month unlock date
    function convertToTransferable() external;
}
```

**Implementation:** BootstrapRewards holds the 75M WOOD allocation. Claimed rewards are tracked as internal balances (not ERC-20 transfers). After 6 months, holders call `convertToTransferable()` to receive actual WOOD tokens. During the non-transferable period, rewards can still be locked into veWOOD (providing governance power) or staked into the Safety Pool.

**Why non-transferable?** This is the critical innovation borrowed from Morpho. During months 1-6, bootstrapping rewards create governance alignment (recipients can lock for veWOOD and vote) without any sell pressure on the WOOD market. By month 6, the protocol should have established revenue, making the token's value grounded in fundamentals before the bootstrapping supply enters circulation.

**After month 12: zero emissions.** The protocol must sustain itself entirely on real fee revenue. If revenue is insufficient to attract participants, that's a signal to improve the product — not to print more tokens.

### 5. veWOOD Governance (Simplified)

veWOOD holders vote on protocol governance. **There are no syndicate gauges or emission direction votes** — the v3 gauge model is removed entirely.

**Governance scope:**

| Action | Quorum | Threshold | Timelock |
|--------|--------|-----------|----------|
| Protocol parameter changes (fee rates, safety pool caps) | 10% of veWOOD | Simple majority | 48 hours |
| Treasury spending proposals | 15% of veWOOD | Simple majority | 72 hours |
| New syndicate approvals (quality gate) | 5% of veWOOD | Simple majority | 24 hours |
| Emergency actions (pause, slash) | 5% of veWOOD | 2/3 supermajority | None |
| Fee distribution ratio changes | 20% of veWOOD | 2/3 supermajority | 7 days |

**Why no gauges?** In Aerodrome, gauges direct emissions to liquidity pools. Bribes arise because protocols need liquidity and will pay for emission direction. In Sherwood, there is no analogous dynamic — syndicate agents don't need to bribe for emissions because their revenue comes from strategy performance fees, not from attracting WOOD emissions. Attempting to force a bribe market creates an artificial game that drains value rather than creating it.

**veWOOD holder earnings summary:**

| Source | Asset | Frequency | Mechanism |
|--------|-------|-----------|-----------|
| Protocol fee share (50%) | USDC/WETH (vault asset) | Each epoch | FeeDistributor claim |
| Bootstrapping rewards (30%) | WOOD (non-transferable → transferable) | Months 1-12 only | BootstrapRewards claim |

### 6. Buyback-and-Lock (BuybackEngine.sol)

**Retained from v3 with modifications.** 20% of protocol fee revenue is used to buy WOOD from the WOOD/WETH pool and lock it as protocol-owned veWOOD.

**Execution via CoW Protocol TWAP:**

```solidity
interface IBuybackEngine {
    /// @notice Execute a buyback using accumulated fee revenue
    /// @param token The fee token to sell (e.g., USDC, WETH)
    /// @param amount The amount to sell
    /// @param minWoodOut Minimum WOOD to receive (slippage protection via 30-min TWAP)
    /// @dev Creates a CoW TWAP order: 24 parts, 1hr intervals
    function executeBuyback(address token, uint256 amount, uint256 minWoodOut) external;

    /// @notice Lock purchased WOOD as protocol veWOOD (auto-max-lock)
    /// @dev Called by CoW post-hook after TWAP order fills
    function lockPurchasedWood() external;

    /// @notice View accumulated fee revenue pending buyback
    function pendingBuyback(address token) external view returns (uint256);

    /// @notice View total WOOD bought and locked historically
    function totalBoughtAndLocked() external view returns (uint256);
}
```

**Why lock instead of burn:** Locked WOOD earns fee revenue share and votes — the protocol treasury becomes a productive governance participant. This also avoids regulatory concerns around token burning (which some jurisdictions view as evidence of securities-like properties).

**Impact modeling:**

| Annual Protocol Revenue | 20% to Buyback | At $0.05 WOOD | Annual WOOD Removed |
|------------------------|----------------|---------------|---------------------|
| $500K | $100K | $0.05 | 2M WOOD (0.4% of supply) |
| $2M | $400K | $0.05 | 8M WOOD (1.6% of supply) |
| $10M | $2M | $0.05 | 40M WOOD (8% of supply) |
| $50M | $10M | $0.05 | 200M WOOD (40% of supply) |

At scale, the buyback becomes a significant supply sink — but only if the protocol generates real revenue.

## Fee Architecture Integration

v4 simplifies the fee architecture by collapsing all new revenue streams into a single FeeDistributor:

### 1. Strategy Profits (vault asset) — existing, unchanged

> Source: `SyndicateGovernor._distributeFees()`

```
Gross Profit (from strategy settlement)
  ├─ Protocol Fee (protocolFeeBps) → FeeDistributor.sol
  ├─ Agent Fee (bps) → proposer + co-proposers
  ├─ Management Fee (bps) → vault owner
  └─ Remainder → vault (depositors)
```

### 2. Fee Distribution (new — replaces emissions)

```
FeeDistributor (each epoch)
  ├─ 50% → veWOOD holders (claim in vault asset)
  ├─ 20% → BuybackEngine (CoW TWAP → veWOOD)
  ├─ 20% → Protocol treasury multisig
  └─ 10% → SafetyPool reward buffer
```

### 3. Bootstrapping Incentives (months 1-12 only)

```
BootstrapRewards (monthly budget, steep decay)
  ├─ 60% → Vault depositors (pro-rata by deposit size × duration)
  ├─ 30% → veWOOD lockers (pro-rata by voting power)
  └─ 10% → Safety stakers (pro-rata by staked amount)
```

**Who earns what (v4):**

| Participant | Vault Asset Profits | Fee Revenue Share | Bootstrapping WOOD | Safety Pool |
|-------------|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| Vault depositors | Remainder after fees | — | 60% (months 1-12) | Protected by first-loss |
| Vault owner | Management fee | — | — | — |
| Agent (proposer) | Performance fee | — | — | — |
| veWOOD holders | — | 50% (real yield) | 30% (months 1-12) | — |
| Safety stakers | — | 10% | 10% (months 1-12) | Enhanced yield |
| Protocol treasury | Protocol fee → FeeDistributor | 20% | — | — |
| Buyback engine | — | 20% (→ locked veWOOD) | — | — |

**Comparison to v3:** v3 had four distinct revenue streams (strategy profits, WOOD emissions, trading fees, bribes) flowing through multiple contracts (Minter, FeeCollector, VoteIncentive, VaultRewardsDistributor, RewardsDistributor). v4 consolidates to two: strategy profits (existing) and fee distribution (new). This reduces contract surface area from 9 to 6, cutting audit scope and bug surface.

## Epoch Lifecycle

The epoch lifecycle is significantly simplified from v3. No gauge voting, no emission minting, no rebase calculation.

```
Thursday 00:00 UTC — Epoch N starts
│
├── FeeDistributor.distributeEpoch(N-1)
│   ├── Collect all protocol fees deposited during epoch N-1
│   ├── Split: 50% veWOOD / 20% buyback / 20% treasury / 10% safety
│   ├── Transfer treasury share to multisig
│   ├── Transfer safety share to SafetyPool reward buffer
│   └── Queue buyback share for BuybackEngine
│
├── BuybackEngine.executeBuyback()
│   ├── Create CoW TWAP order (24 parts, 1hr intervals)
│   └── Post-hook: lock purchased WOOD as veWOOD (auto-max-lock)
│
├── BootstrapRewards.updateEpoch() [months 1-12 only]
│   ├── Calculate epoch's reward budget (from decay schedule)
│   └── Make rewards claimable for depositors/lockers/stakers
│
├── veWOOD holders claim epoch N-1 fee rewards (in USDC/WETH)
├── Vault depositors claim epoch N-1 bootstrapping rewards [months 1-12]
├── Safety stakers claim accumulated rewards
├── Governance votes on any active proposals
│
Wednesday 23:59 UTC — Epoch N ends
```

**Permissionless epoch flip:** `distributeEpoch()` is callable by anyone after the epoch boundary. A keeper bot or any user can trigger it. Small gas refund incentivizes timely execution.

## Security Considerations

1. **Reentrancy:** VotingEscrow handles NFTs and token transfers — use ReentrancyGuard on all external calls. SafetyPool's stake/unstake/slash flow must be reentrancy-safe (CEI pattern + ReentrancyGuard).

2. **Flash loan attacks:** Voting power based on locked balance (not transferable), immune to flash loans. Safety Pool staking has a 14-day cooldown — flash-stake is impossible.

3. **Checkpoint manipulation:** Use `block.timestamp` checkpoints for vote weight snapshots. FeeDistributor must snapshot veWOOD balances at epoch boundary, not at claim time.

4. **Oracle manipulation:** WOOD/WETH pool price can be manipulated via flash loans. **Never use spot pool price on-chain.** All price-dependent operations (SafetyPool slashing, BuybackEngine) use a 30-minute TWAP via Uniswap V3's `observe()`.

5. **Safety Pool slashing atomicity:** Slash operations must be atomic — partial slashes (reverting mid-way) could leave the pool in an inconsistent state. Use a single storage update pattern: calculate slash amount, update pool balance, then transfer.

6. **Safety Pool bank run:** The 14-day cooldown prevents bank runs but creates a different risk: if stakers see a large pending slash, they cannot exit. This is by design — the cooldown IS the insurance mechanism. However, the 30% max slash per epoch provides staker protection.

7. **Fee token handling:** FeeDistributor must use SafeERC20 for all transfers. Explicitly reject fee-on-transfer and rebasing tokens (or account for them). Whitelist accepted fee tokens via governance.

8. **BuybackEngine MEV protection:** CoW TWAP orders are MEV-resistant by design (batch auction settlement). The `minWoodOut` parameter provides additional slippage protection. If CoW is unavailable on Base at launch, fall back to a keeper-based splitter with multisig execution rights (documented as a trust assumption).

9. **Non-transferable token edge cases:** BootstrapRewards non-transferable balances must not be usable as collateral, transferable via approval, or flashable. Internal accounting only — no ERC-20 balance until `convertToTransferable()` is called after 6 months.

10. **Access control:** All pausable contracts use a 2-of-3 multisig as pause guardian. Unpausing requires a 3-of-5 protocol multisig to prevent premature restart. SafetyPool.slash() is callable only by SyndicateGovernor — this is a critical access control.

11. **Upgradeability:** All WOOD contracts are deployed as **immutable** (not UUPS upgradeable). VotingEscrow holds locked tokens — upgradeability would introduce rug risk. Migration path for bugs: (a) pause affected contract, (b) deploy new contract, (c) snapshot state, (d) users migrate via claim mechanism.

12. **Overflow:** veWOOD voting power calculation uses time math — careful with uint256 overflow at boundaries. FeeDistributor division must handle rounding correctly (round down, accumulate dust in a sweep-able buffer).

## Regulatory Considerations

> **This section is not legal advice. It identifies risks and recommends actions.**

### Howey Test Analysis (US Securities Law)

| Prong | v3 Assessment | v4 Assessment | Change |
|-------|--------------|--------------|--------|
| **Investment of money** | Met (buy/earn WOOD, lock it) | Met (same) | No change |
| **Common enterprise** | Likely met (token value tied to protocol) | Likely met (same) | No change |
| **Expectation of profits** | Arguably met (trading fees, bribes, rebase) | **Higher risk** (explicit fee revenue share is closer to a dividend) | ⚠ Increased risk |
| **Solely from efforts of others** | Key mitigating factor (active voting/governance) | **Stronger mitigation** (governance directly controls fee parameters, safety pool caps, syndicate approvals) | ✅ Improved |

**v4 regulatory trade-offs:**

The explicit revenue sharing model (50% of fees to veWOOD) is more transparent but also more dividend-like than v3's indirect value accrual (emissions + bribes). This is a conscious trade-off:

- **Pro:** Honest framing. The token's value proposition is clear and auditable. No obfuscation through complex emission/bribe mechanics.
- **Con:** Regulators may view direct fee distribution as stronger evidence of a security.
- **Mitigation:** veWOOD holders must actively participate in governance (vote on parameters, approve syndicates, manage safety pool). Fee distribution is tied to governance participation, not passive holding. **Consider requiring a minimum governance participation threshold to earn fee revenue** (e.g., must have voted in at least 1 proposal in the last 4 epochs).

**Improvements over v3:**
- No inflationary emissions (removes "profiting from token appreciation driven by artificial demand")
- Non-transferable bootstrapping rewards reduce "investment contract" framing
- Safety staking provides clear utility (insurance function) beyond speculation
- No bribe layer removes the optics of paying for token direction

### Recommendations

1. **Engage securities counsel** before TGE — the fee revenue share model needs explicit legal sign-off
2. **Geographic restrictions** — consider excluding US persons from public sale/LBP
3. **Governance participation requirement** — implement minimum voting activity threshold for fee revenue eligibility (strengthens "not solely from efforts of others" defense)
4. **Utility-first messaging** — position WOOD as governance/insurance token, not yield-bearing asset
5. **Avoid profit language** — use "protocol fee share", "governance rewards", not "yield", "dividends", "returns"
6. **Safety staking framing** — position as "protocol insurance participation" not "staking for yield"
7. **SAFT structure** for any pre-sale allocations

## Downside Scenario Planning

### Scenario 1: Low Protocol Revenue (< $100K/year)

**What breaks:** veWOOD fee share is negligible. At $100K annual revenue and 50% to veWOOD holders, total veWOOD yield is $50K/year. If 100M WOOD is locked ($5M at $0.05/WOOD), that's 1% APR — not attractive enough to lock.

**Why this is different from v3:** In v3, low revenue didn't matter because emissions provided artificial yield. In v4, low revenue means low token utility — period. This is by design. The token SHOULD have low value if the protocol doesn't generate revenue.

**Responses:**
1. **Bootstrapping incentives (months 1-12)** provide supplemental yield during the revenue ramp period
2. **Safety staking** provides utility independent of fee revenue (insurance function)
3. **Focus on product** — improve strategy performance, onboard more agents, grow TVL. The token's job is to reflect protocol value, not create it.
4. **Treasury runway:** 50M WOOD (10%) + 25M reserve (5%) provides 18+ months of operational funding even with zero fee revenue

### Scenario 2: WOOD Price Decline (>50%)

**What breaks:** Safety Pool's WOOD-denominated coverage becomes less valuable in dollar terms. If WOOD drops 50%, the safety pool's coverage ratio halves.

**Responses:**
1. **Dynamic coverage ratio:** If coverage ratio falls below minimum (5% of TVL), new deposits are paused until coverage recovers
2. **Protocol treasury deploys additional WOOD to safety pool** from reserve allocation
3. **No emission-based circuit breaker needed** — there are no emissions to cut. The protocol's cost structure is fixed (team vesting is predetermined, bootstrapping schedule is predetermined)
4. **Buyback becomes more efficient** — 20% of fee revenue buys more WOOD at lower prices, creating counter-cyclical buy pressure

### Scenario 3: Safety Pool Slash Event

**What breaks:** A strategy loses significant capital. Safety Pool absorbs losses. Stakers' WOOD is sold to cover depositors.

**Responses:**
1. **30% max slash per epoch** prevents complete pool wipeout
2. **Event is public and transparent** — builds trust that the insurance mechanism works
3. **Post-slash incentive:** Remaining stakers earn a higher share of rewards (same reward pool, fewer stakers). This attracts new stakers to rebuild coverage.
4. **Governance review:** Large slash events trigger mandatory governance review of the syndicate/strategy that caused the loss

### Scenario 4: Bootstrap Period Ends, Revenue Insufficient

**What breaks:** Month 13 arrives, bootstrapping incentives end, but protocol revenue isn't enough to sustain veWOOD yields that attract locking.

**Responses:**
1. **This is the honest outcome.** If the protocol can't generate enough revenue in 12 months to sustain its token economy, it should not be propped up by inflation.
2. **Governance can vote to extend bootstrapping** using treasury/reserve WOOD — but this is a conscious decision, not an automatic mechanism. The protocol must make the case that more time will lead to revenue.
3. **Minimum viable state:** Even with zero fee revenue, the protocol still functions. Vaults still operate. Strategies still execute. Agents still earn performance fees. The token just doesn't have yield — which is honest pricing.

### Scenario 5: Safety Pool Insufficient for Large Loss

**What breaks:** A strategy loses more than the Safety Pool can cover. Depositors absorb the excess loss.

**Responses:**
1. **This is known and documented.** Safety Pool provides first-loss protection, not full insurance. Depositors are informed of the coverage ratio before depositing.
2. **Coverage ratio transparency:** Displayed prominently in the UI for each syndicate
3. **Governance can set higher coverage requirements** for riskier strategies
4. **Post-event:** Protocol evaluates whether the strategy type should be restricted

## Phased Deployment Plan

Reduced from 5 phases (v3) to 3 phases. Fewer contracts = faster deployment.

### Phase 0: Token Launch

- Deploy `WoodToken.sol` (LayerZero OFT, 500M minted at genesis)
- Execute initial distribution (all allocations to respective addresses/contracts)
- Run LBP or public sale (100M WOOD)
- Seed WOOD/WETH Uniswap V3 pool (50M WOOD + equivalent WETH)
- Seed shareToken/WOOD pools for existing syndicates
- **Gate:** Token is live and liquid on at least one DEX. WOOD/WETH pool has >$100K liquidity.

### Phase 1: Core Tokenomics

- Deploy `VotingEscrow.sol` — enable WOOD locking for veWOOD
- Deploy `FeeDistributor.sol` — begin collecting protocol fees and distributing to veWOOD holders
- Deploy `BootstrapRewards.sol` — start bootstrapping incentive distribution
- Deploy `BuybackEngine.sol` — begin fee-funded buybacks
- **Gate:** >15% of supply locked as veWOOD, fee distribution working for 4+ epochs, buyback executed successfully at least once
- **If gate not met within 8 weeks — Path A:** Increase bootstrapping rewards front-loading (pull from months 4-6 budget into months 1-3). Reduce veWOOD lock minimum to 2 weeks temporarily.
- **If gate still not met by week 16 — Path B:** Enter maintenance mode. Pause bootstrapping rewards. The protocol continues operating without token incentives until organic demand justifies resumption.

### Phase 2: Safety & Insurance

- Deploy `SafetyPool.sol` — enable WOOD staking for first-loss insurance
- Seed Safety Pool with 50M WOOD from allocation
- Connect SafetyPool.slash() to SyndicateGovernor settlement flow
- Enable safety staker rewards in FeeDistributor
- **Gate:** Safety Pool has >$500K equivalent coverage. Slash mechanism tested on testnet with simulated losses. Coverage ratio displayed in UI.

**Emergency infrastructure:** All contracts include OpenZeppelin Pausable. Pause guardian (2-of-3 multisig) can halt any contract. Unpause requires 3-of-5 multisig.

**Testnet deployment (before each phase):** Full deployment on Base Sepolia with simulated epochs, fee generation, slashing, and bootstrapping reward distribution.

## Open Questions

1. **WOOD token launch mechanism:** Same options as v3 (Fjord Foundry LBP recommended, direct Uniswap pool as fallback). Decision: TBD.

2. **Governance participation requirement for fee revenue:** Should veWOOD holders be required to vote in at least N proposals per M epochs to be eligible for fee distribution? This strengthens the "active governance" regulatory defense but reduces UX simplicity. **Recommendation:** Require voting in at least 1 proposal per 4 epochs (monthly). Non-voters' fee share is redirected to the buyback engine.

3. **Safety Pool slash oracle:** How is the loss amount determined on-chain? Currently, `SyndicateGovernor._distributeFees()` calculates gross profit — a negative settlement implies a loss. Need to verify that the settlement flow correctly identifies and quantifies losses for the slash trigger. **Recommendation:** Add a `settlementResult` return value to the governor's settlement function that explicitly reports profit or loss amount.

4. **Safety Pool asset denomination:** Safety Pool holds WOOD, but coverage is needed in vault asset (USDC/WETH). Slashing requires selling WOOD on market — large slashes could cause significant price impact. **Recommendation:** Cap individual slash at min(30% of pool, amount that would move WOOD price >5% based on pool depth). Excess losses are socialized to depositors.

5. **Multi-token fee distribution UX:** Different syndicates settle in different assets. veWOOD holders may need to claim USDC from one syndicate's fees and WETH from another's. **Recommendation:** FeeDistributor offers `claimAll()` that sweeps all tokens in one transaction. Consider auto-converting to a single asset (USDC) via DEX aggregator in the claim flow.

6. **BootstrapRewards lock-while-non-transferable:** Can non-transferable bootstrapping WOOD be locked into veWOOD before the 6-month unlock? **Recommendation:** Yes — this is desirable behavior. It creates governance alignment without sell pressure. BootstrapRewards should have a `lockIntoVeWOOD()` function that creates a veWOOD position directly.

7. **Multi-chain:** Base only initially, or plan for L2 expansion? WOOD token is LayerZero OFT (cross-chain ready). veWOOD, FeeDistributor, and SafetyPool should stay on Base. Cross-chain fee collection would require bridging infrastructure.

8. **Audit scope:** 6 contracts (reduced from 9). Estimated audit: 4-6 weeks for a tier-1 auditor. SafetyPool slashing logic and FeeDistributor accounting are the highest-risk areas.

9. **Safety Pool seed source:** The 50M WOOD allocated to safety pool seeding — who stakes it? Options: (a) protocol stakes it directly (protocol-owned first-loss), (b) sell to early stakers at a discount, (c) distribute as locked staking positions to LBP participants. **Recommendation:** Protocol stakes directly. This demonstrates confidence in the strategies and provides immediate depositor protection.

10. **Fee distribution frequency:** Weekly epochs may be too infrequent for fee distribution during high-activity periods. Consider allowing mid-epoch fee deposits that are distributed at epoch flip. **Recommendation:** Keep weekly epochs for simplicity. Fees accumulate in FeeDistributor and are distributed once per epoch.
